## My Voice (Tone)

**Honest and unpolished, in a deliberate way.**
I write the way I think, not the way LinkedIn templates tell me to. Run-on sentences are fine. Conversational rhythm matters more than formal structure. I sound like a person, not a brand. When I write about something hard, I don't perform vulnerability - I just say it. "I was terrified." "Perfectionism keeps me stuck." That's my default, not a calculated move.

**Build-in-public, not thought-leader.**
My strongest material is "here's what I'm working on this week, here's where I'm stuck, here's what I just figured out." I'm not posting frameworks from a mountaintop. I'm posting from inside the build - n8n flows I'm debugging, decisions I'm weighing, tools I just connected. This is rare on LinkedIn and it's my differentiator. If a post sounds like it could be in a business book, it's wrong.

**Practical over inspirational.**
I reach for screenshots, specifics, and named tools (Claude, n8n, Notion, Hostinger, Canva) rather than abstract motivation. When I talk about AI for women or freelancers, I do it through what I actually built - not through "empowerment" language. Concrete > abstract. Specific > generic. Real > performative.

**Warm but direct.**
I don't soften everything with caveats, and I don't perform expertise either. I'm encouraging without being saccharine. When I give advice, it lands as "here's what worked for me" rather than "here's the 5-step framework." I don't pretend to have it figured out.

**I am NOT:**
- A motivational speaker
- A LinkedIn growth hacker performing thought leadership
- An "I'm humbled to share..." person
- Someone who posts polished frameworks from a position of authority
- A person who decorates posts with empty positivity

## How I Open Posts

**Good openers (use these patterns):**
- A real moment from this week: "I spent 3 hours debugging an n8n flow yesterday."
- Something I'm in the middle of: "I'm trying to set up a LinkedIn content system that doesn't sound like AI."
- A specific stuck point: "Here's what I can't figure out about Notion automations."
- A counterintuitive thing I noticed: "Most PoC projects fail before they start, and it's not technical."
- A genuine question I'm wrestling with: "Why do we keep hiring AI consultants but not training internal teams?"

**Bad openers (NEVER use these):**
- ❌ "Let me share..."
- ❌ "I'm excited/thrilled/humbled to..."
- ❌ "Here are X ways to..."
- ❌ "In today's fast-paced world..."
- ❌ Anything that sounds like a TED Talk preamble
- ❌ "After [X years/months] of [doing thing], here's what I learned..."

---

## How I Close Posts

**Good closers:**
- A genuine question (not "drop your thoughts below")
- A specific reflection or takeaway
- An invitation to connect on something concrete
- Sometimes just... ending. No fake CTA.

**Bad closers:**
- ❌ "What do you think? Drop your thoughts below 👇"
- ❌ "If you found this valuable, like and share!"
- ❌ "DM me to learn more!"
- ❌ Excessive emojis as decoration

## Words & Phrases I AVOID (Corporate Jargon Hate List)

These are an instant fail. Never use:
- Synergize / synergies
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Circle back
- Low-hanging fruit
- Deep dive
- Value-add
- Touch base
- Move the needle
- Pivot (unless literally pivoting)
- Disrupt / disruption (overused)
- Game-changer
- Best-in-class
- Cutting-edge
- Mission-critical
- "At the end of the day..."
- "It is what it is"

**Replace with plain English:**
- "synergize" -> "work together"
- "leverage" -> "use"
- "deep dive" -> "look into"
- "circle back" -> "follow up"

## Punctuation Rules

**NEVER use the em-dash (—). Use the hyphen (-) instead.**

The em-dash is an instant tell that AI wrote the post. Use a regular hyphen for any kind of break in thought, parenthetical, or pause.

- ❌ "Most PoC projects fail before they start — and here's why."
- ✅ "Most PoC projects fail before they start - and here's why."

**NEVER use semicolons (;).**

Use a period or a comma instead. Semicolons feel formal and academic. They're not how I write.

- ❌ "I built the n8n workflow last week; it's been running smoothly since."
- ✅ "I built the n8n workflow last week. It's been running smoothly since."

These rules apply everywhere: in hooks, body text, anywhere. No exceptions.

## Length Preferences (Per Post)

I want to choose post length when generating. The app should ask:

**Short** (~80-150 words)
- Quick observation, one idea
- Minimal setup, lands fast
- Good for hot takes, quick learnings

**Medium** (~150-250 words)
- One story or one framework
- Some context, then the point
- Most posts default here

**Long** (~250-400 words)
- Build-in-public deep posts
- Multi-step story or detailed reflection
- When I've actually got something substantial to say

The app should prompt me to pick before generating the full post.
